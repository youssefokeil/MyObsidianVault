## 3) Special Case: Perfect Square
1. *Check* the trinomial is perfect square
	1. First term is perfect square
	2. Middle term is = ($\pm2 \times \sqrt{\text{first term}}\times \sqrt{\text{third term}}$)
	3. Third Term is a perfect square
	
	Example: $4x^{2}+12x+9$
	4. first term is perfect square $\sqrt{4x^{2}}=2x$
	5. Middle term: $2 \times \sqrt{4x^{2}} \times \sqrt{9}= 2 \times 2x \times 3 = 12x$
	6. Third term is a perfect square: $\sqrt{9}=3$
	**Then the number is a perfect square**
2. If the number is a perfect square, *factorize* like below (factorize بعد ما اتأكدت)
	$(\sqrt{\text{first term}} \pm \sqrt{\text{third term}})^2$
	الsign اللي في النص ليها علاقة بالsign بتاعت الmiddle term
	For our example: $4x^{2}+12x+9$
	**factorization:** 	$(\sqrt{4x^{2}} + \sqrt{9})^{2}= (2x+3)^2$
