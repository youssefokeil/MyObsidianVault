# Working Class before the Industrial Revolution
Before the [[proletariat]], we had slaves, serfs and handi-craftsmen. 


|                       | slaves                                                                      | serfs                                                                                                                                                          | handi-craftsmen                                                                                           | Manufacturing workers                                                                                                                       | [[proletariat]]                                                                             |

| **Time period**       | in antiquity                                                                | under feudalism                                                                                                                                                | before the 18th century and still exists to some capacity                                                 | $16^{th}$ to $18^{th}$ century.                                                                                                             | after the industrial revolution, late $18^{th}$ century.                                    |
| **Role**              | Typically, works in the land/house of the owner.                            | possesses and uses a mean of production, a piece of land, he cultivates that land. There was also pledge that the serf does whatever the lord wants you to do. | Has his tools and sells the work he does. They create hand-made crafts. They then sell it.                | works in a factory. The factory depends on his hand labor.                                                                                  | lives entirely from sale of labor-power, and doesn't draw profits from any kind of capital. |
| **How does he live?** | Assured an existence, however miserable, because of the master's interests. | In exchange of the cultivation of the land, they're given part of the land for their sustenance.                                                               | His goal is to acquire capital himself to exploit other workers.
